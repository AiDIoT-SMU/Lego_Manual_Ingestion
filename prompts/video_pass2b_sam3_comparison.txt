**STEP 2: PART IDENTIFICATION OF THE CHANGED REGION**
Now analyze ONLY the part that changed:

**CRITICAL: STUD COUNTING METHODOLOGY**
Studs are the small circular bumps on top of LEGO bricks. Count them carefully:

- **Look at the TOP SURFACE** of the new part
- **Count visible circular studs** one by one:
  * 2 studs in a LINE = 1x2
  * 4 studs in a SQUARE (2x2 grid) = 2x2
  * 6 studs in a LINE = 1x6
  * 8 studs (2 rows of 4, forming 2x4 grid) = 2x4
  * 3 studs in a LINE = 1x3

- **For tiles** (smooth top with NO studs visible):
  * Compare the tile's footprint to adjacent bricks with visible studs
  * If it covers the same area as a brick with 4 studs, it's a 2x2 tile

- **If partially obscured**:
  * Count the visible studs
  * Estimate based on brick shape and symmetry
  * Note partial visibility in your reasoning

**PART PROPERTIES:**
- **Color**: What color is the new part? (be specific: "dark grey", "light grey", "white", "black", etc.)
- **Type**:
  * **brick**: thick piece with studs on top (standard LEGO brick)
  * **plate**: thin piece with studs on top (1/3 height of brick)
  * **tile**: smooth top with NO studs visible
  * **special piece**: unusual shapes, rounded pieces, etc.

- **Size**: Based on stud count
  * Format: [width]x[length] (e.g., "2x2" for 4 studs in square, "1x2" for 2 studs in line)
  * If you count 4 studs arranged in a 2x2 square → size is "2x2"
  * If you count 2 studs arranged in a line → size is "1x2"

- **Stud Count**: The exact number of circular studs you counted on the top surface
