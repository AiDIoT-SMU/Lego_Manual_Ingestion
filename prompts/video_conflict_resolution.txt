You are resolving conflicts between a LEGO manual's part list and video evidence.

MANUAL SAYS:
Step {step_number} requires: {manual_parts}

VIDEO EVIDENCE:
In frames {frame_range} (timestamps {time_range}), we detected: {video_parts}
Detection confidence scores: {confidence_scores}

REFERENCE IMAGES:
You will see:
1. Manual part images (cropped from the instruction manual)
2. Video frame crops showing the parts detected in the user's hands/workspace

TASK:
Determine which source (manual or video) is correct and explain why.

ANALYSIS CRITERIA:

1. Video Quality:
   - Can we clearly see the parts in the video frames?
   - Are hands or other objects occluding the parts?
   - Is the lighting sufficient for accurate identification?

2. Detection Confidence:
   - Confidence >0.8 is highly reliable
   - Confidence 0.6-0.8 is moderately reliable
   - Confidence <0.6 is uncertain

3. Multiple Frame Confirmation:
   - Is the part seen consistently across multiple frames?
   - Parts visible in 3+ frames with high confidence are very reliable

4. Visual Similarity:
   - Could the detected part be misidentified?
   - Are there similar-looking parts that might be confused?
   - Check color, size, and shape carefully

5. Manual Extraction Errors:
   - Manual data comes from 2D image analysis which can make mistakes
   - Common errors: misidentifying colors from perspective/lighting
   - Common errors: confusing similar-sized parts (1x2 vs 2x2)
   - Common errors: missing parts that are small or partially visible

6. Context:
   - Does the video part make sense for this assembly step?
   - Is the manual part list consistent with the subassembly image?

Return ONLY valid JSON in this exact format:

If conflict exists:

  "correct_source": "video",
  "correct_parts": ["red round tile", "white 1x2 brick"],

  "confidence": 0.92
}

{

{
  "conflict_exists": true,
  "correct_source": "uncertain",

Fields explanation:
- conflict_exists: true if manual and video disagree, false if they match
- correct_source: "video", "manual", "both", or "uncertain"

- incorrect_parts: Array of parts that are confirmed incorrect

- confidence: 0.0-1.0 indicating certainty of your conclusion
